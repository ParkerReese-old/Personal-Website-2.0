University of Saskatchewan

I have completed three years in Computer Science at the University of Saskatchewan. Throughout my time at the University I have learned a lot of hard skills. Languages such as Java, C/C++, JavaScript and concepts such as OOP, MVC, and user experience, are things we all had to learn to make our way through. There are, however, a number of indirect skills that I acquired that I’ve found to be equally valuable. Things like time management, communication/collaboration, and knowing when to take a break, are all things I’m glad University has taught me.

Vendasta

Vendasta is the company I got accepted to work for as a Student Software Developer from May 1st, 2017 – September 1st, 2018. The experience and knowledge from University pales in comparison to what I’ve gained from working at Vendasta. After working for over a year I can comfortable describe myself as a full-stack web developer. With a strong understanding of Typescript, Angular, RXJS, Python, and Go, I feel as though I can solve any task.

Vendasta has also made me become more comfortable with myself as a developer. Asking a “stupid” question is no longer the height of embarrassment, but rather something I might do on purpose. Team work, prioritization, and an agile work environment are no longer concepts, but fully understood and utilized daily drivers. I’ve also gotten the opportunity to do public speaking in front of the entire company on multiple occasions. Doing this still scares the socks off of me, but I like to think I’m getting better.
